Reimbursements and P-Card Guidelines

Use of the P-Card for lab expenses is generally highly recommended. Because of tax reporting, inventory, and regulatory requirements, and to simplify reconciliation, the Purchasing Card cannot be used in the following circumstances:

  • Personal Expenses
  • Fabrications
  • Hazardous Materials
  • Travel
  • Student Conference Registration
  • Cash Advances
  • Laboratory Animals
  • Purchases paid via PayPal
  • Purchases on Ebay
  • Any Purchase Over $4,999.99 (Note: The item cost includes tax, shipping, handling and installation, if applicable).
For further information about these restrictions, contact Renee.

While use of the P-Card is strongly encouraged whenever possible, most small expenses (including lab meeting food) can also be charged to personal cards. Whenever submitting a reimbursement request for a non-travel payment made with a personal card, you should provide Renee with a completed copy of the expense certification form signed by Marco. For travel expenses, see below.

ASL Bibliography

When writing a paper, you should use the lab's shared bibliography, hosted on our SVN repository (Segovia) under /bib/. There are strict guidelines on how to add papers to the bibliography. You may find the full guidelines on SVN: a (possibly outdated) version of the guidelines can be found here. Here are the ten cardinal rules of the ASL bibliography:

  • Use main.bib for non-ASL references, and use ASL_papers.bib for ASL references.
  • Use Jabref 3.8.X with ASL lab preferences imported.
  • Check references marked \color{red} by going back to the original source.
  • Use the actual documents or publisherâ€™s website to gather information.
  • Use your SUNet ID name to mark yourself owner of new or modified entries.
  • Use the correct entry types.
  • Properly format fields and strings.
  • Use only strings for month, journal, publisher, institution, organization, and school fields (also booktitle for @inproceedings and title for @proceedings).
  • Autogenerate bibtexkeys, but do not regenerate bibtexkeys for entries that are no longer marked \color{red}.
  • Do not take entries marked \color{red} as an indication of how to choose entry types and format fields. In most cases they are wrong.

NEW: If you have just submitted a paper to a conference or journal, and need the reference updated or added to the lab's publication page, please use this Google form to notify the webmaster.
